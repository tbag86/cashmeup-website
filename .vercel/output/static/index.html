What is a PCP claim?

A PCP (Personal Contract Purchase) claim relates to the widespread mis-selling of car finance in the UK. If you had PCP or HP car finance between roughly 2007 and 2021, your lender may have secretly paid your car dealer a hidden commission that led you to pay more interest than you should have. The financial regulator found this practice to be widespread, and millions of consumers may be owed compensation — potentially £1,000s each.

How do I know if I'm eligible for a PCP claim?

You may be eligible if you took out a PCP or HP (Hire Purchase) car finance agreement between 2007 and 2021 in the UK. The key question is whether your dealer received a discretionary commission from the lender — a practice that was banned by the regulator in January 2021. Most people who had car finance during this period could be eligible. You can check your eligibility on our PCP Eligibility page.

How long does a PCP claim take?

PCP claims are currently taking longer than usual due to the regulator's industry-wide review, which paused the standard 8-week complaint response window. The regulator is expected to provide final guidance in 2025, after which claims should progress more quickly. Our partners will keep you updated throughout the process.

Do I need any documents or paperwork to start a PCP claim?

No. You don't need to dig out old paperwork to begin. Simply fill in our quick form with your basic details and our partners can begin tracing your finance agreements. They have the tools and expertise to locate your agreements even if you don't have the original documents.
